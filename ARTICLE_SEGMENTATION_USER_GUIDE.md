# Article Segmentation - User Guide

## What is Article Segmentation?

Article Segmentation automatically breaks long articles into smaller, manageable parts (typically 300-700 words each). This makes it easier to read and learn from lengthy content without feeling overwhelmed.

## Tips & Tricks

### 1. Use Keyboard Shortcuts

Navigate faster by using arrow keys instead of clicking buttons.

### 2. Review by Part

When reviewing vocabulary:

- Go to Vocabulary mode to see all words from all parts
- Return to Reading mode to review words part-by-part

### 3. Focus on One Section

Don't try to learn everything at once. Focus on mastering one part before moving to the next.

### 4. Check Part Numbers

The part indicator helps you track progress through long articles. Use it to plan your learning sessions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: How are articles divided into parts?

**A**: Articles are automatically segmented into parts of approximately 500 words each, respecting paragraph boundaries.

### Q: Can I jump to a specific part?

**A**: Currently, you can only navigate sequentially using Previous/Next buttons. A "jump to part" feature may be added in the future.

### Q: What happens to my highlights when I navigate?

**A**: Your highlights are saved with the part where you created them. They'll reappear when you return to that part.

### Q: Can I see all vocabulary from all parts?

**A**: Yes! Switch to "Vocabulary" mode (click the ğŸ“ tab or press 'v') to see all vocabulary items from the entire article.

### Q: Why can't I navigate during editing?

**A**: Navigation is disabled while editing a paragraph to prevent accidental data loss. Save or cancel your edit first.

### Q: How long is my position saved?

**A**: Your current part position is saved for 1 hour. After that, the article will open at Part 1.

## Troubleshooting

### Navigation buttons are disabled

- **Previous button disabled**: You're on the first part
- **Next button disabled**: You're on the last part
- **Both buttons disabled**: You're editing a paragraph (save or cancel first)

### Position not restored

- More than 1 hour has passed since you last viewed the article
- Browser session storage was cleared
- You're viewing a different article

### Cards not showing

- Make sure you're in Reading mode (not Vocabulary or Sentences mode)
- Check that you've highlighted words/sentences in the current part
- Try navigating to a different part and back

## Related Features

- **Highlight Manager**: Highlight vocabulary and sentences in each part
- **Edit Mode**: Edit paragraph content within each part
- **TTS (Text-to-Speech)**: Listen to content from the current part
- **Context Menus**: Right-click options work within each part
